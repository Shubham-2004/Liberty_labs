Know Your Right

The fundamental rights guaranteed to Indian citizens by the Constitution include the right to equality, right to freedom, right against exploitation, right to freedom of religion, cultural and educational rights, and right to constitutional remedies.

T Yes, fundamental rights can be suspended during emergencies declared under Article 352 of the Constitution, which includes national emergency, state emergency, and financial emergency. However, some rights such as right to life and personal liberty (Article 21) cannot be suspended.

Article 21 of the Indian Constitution guarantees the right to life and personal liberty, which is considered the most important right. It ensures that no person shall be deprived of his/her life or personal liberty except according to the procedure established by law.

While Indian citizens have the freedom of speech and expression, it is subject to certain reasonable restrictions such as defamation, contempt of court, decency, morality, security of the state, etc., as specified under Article 19(2) of the Constitution.

Indian citizens have the right to privacy, which has been recognized as a fundamental right by the Supreme Court of India in the landmark judgment of Justice K.S. Puttaswamy (Retd.) vs Union of India. The right to privacy is protected under Article 21 of the Constitution.

Indian citizens have the freedom to practice, profess, and propagate any religion of their choice under Article 25 of the Constitution. However, the state can regulate religious activities to maintain public order, morality, and health.

Various provisions such as Articles 29 and 30 of the Constitution safeguard the rights of minorities in India, including the right to conserve their language, script, and culture, and the right to establish and administer educational institutions of their choice.

Several laws and provisions have been enacted to protect the rights of women in India, including the Protection of Women from Domestic Violence Act, 2005, Dowry Prohibition Act, 1961, and laws against sexual harassment in the workplace.
